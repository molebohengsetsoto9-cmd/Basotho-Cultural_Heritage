Traditional music

Basotho music features instruments like the lekolulo(flute)and setolo-tolo(jaw harp).These are used in celebrations and storytelling

Traditional dance

mokhibo

A greaceful,kneeling dance perforemed by women. Emphasizes shoulder and upper body movements ,often accompanied by singing and ulilation, reflecting femininity and community pride

litolobonya

a greceful women's dance usually done during festivals and celebration.

mohobelo(warrior dance)

this dance is perfomed by men,showcasing power,rhythm,and coordination.prforemed during initiation ceremonies and nation celebration.symbolizes strength,unity,and Basotho warrior tradition

ndlamo

A stomping style dance performed by botho men and women common in festive occation. Features energetic footwork and traditional chants.

motjeko

A group dance that combines rhythm,hand clapping,and traditional drumming.Often seen in weddings and communal gatherings.

seboko

performed during cultural events where individual recite theirclan praises(seboko).

phathisi

A men's dance involving rhythmic stamping while wearing bells or leggings with tassels.Often performed with and singing.

Lets'oele

A community dance often performed during weddings or village celebration .Include call and response singing and coodinated movement in circle.

selete dance

Performed by both men and women during initiation and riyal ceremonies.Involve strong footwork and arm movements,usually accompanied by chanting.

Boroso dance

A humorous and entertaining dance that include playful gestures and mimicry.Performed diring village social gatherings or informal celebrations.

khoeli ea basali(women's moon month)

Done by older wemen during moolit evenings.It involves strorytelling through movements and communal singing

mohope dancce

A courtship or celebraory dance often performed by youth.Dancers wear beads,blanket and traditional attire.

Traditional musical instruments

Lekolulo(flute)

made from reed, played mainly by herd boys.produces soft, melodic tones and is used for stortelling or self-entertainment in the fields

setolo-tolo(jaw harp)

A mouth resonated intruments made from metal or bamboo.produces twangingsound and is used in solo performence.

Thomo

A traditional string instrument used mainly by women.played by plucking strings atteched to a wooden or gourd frame.

morapa(drumb)

A large traditional drunm used during dances,ceremonies and public gatherings.sets the rhythm and energy for most traditional dances.

mamokhorong

A one-string bowen instrument similar to a violin.produces deep vibrating sounds and often used for folk music

lesiba

A wind instrunent made from a quill and string attached to a stick. produces by blowing and plucking,traditionally used by herders

ntsiketsi

A type of whistle made from animal horn or bone.produces sharp tones to signal or accompany music

khali

A percussion instrument made from wood or metal that produces a rattling sound when shaken.Adds rhythm and texture to dance performence

mokhope

made from bottle caps srung together or seeds inside a container. used by women to play lillabies or praise song

Rakatla

made from bottle caps strung together or seeds inside a container.shaken like maracas to add rhythm,

mokhebo(Horn trumpet)

used in the past to announce importance events or gatherings.Now mainly seen in ceremonial reenactments
